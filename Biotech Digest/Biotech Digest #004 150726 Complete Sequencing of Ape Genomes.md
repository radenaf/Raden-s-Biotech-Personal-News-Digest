# Biotech Digest #004 150726 Complet Sequencing of Ape Genomes

**Date:** 150726
**Paper:** Complete sequencing of ape genomes 
**Journal:**  Nature volume 641, pages 401–418 (2025)
**Link/DOI:**  https://www.nature.com/articles/s41586-025-08816-3

---

## TL;DR
Normal Ape Genome studies would often stack them against the Human Genome thus causing a bias in mind while conducting the study. This study uses both X and Y chromosomes when compared to other studies using only Y. Six ape species were looked at: chimpanzee, bonobo, gorilla, Bornean orangutan, Sumatran orangutan and siamang.

---

## Key Takeaways
- There are major differences in MHC and immunoglobulin loci between Apes.
- There were substantial amount of manual curation in the data set. 
- Study is broken to 3 parts: generation of assemblies, added value to evolutionary analyses, and insights into unassembled regions.
- Most rapid evolved region being the MHC and the chromosome
- Higher levels of divergence were seen compared to previous estimations
- Ape NORs exist on different chromosomes for each species
- Analyses revealed new candidate genes and regons to account for phenotypic differences among apes.
